2. What is JPG vs. WebP? Defining the Contenders

Before we pit them against each other, let's understand what each format was designed to do.

JPG (or JPEG): The Established Workhorse

JPEG, standing for Joint Photographic Experts Group, was created in 1992. Its primary goal was to achieve high compression for photographic images with minimal perceptible loss in quality. It uses a "lossy" compression algorithm, which means it permanently discards data the human eye is less likely to notice—especially in areas of fine detail and smooth color transitions.

Why JPG Still Matters: Its universal support is its superpower. Every single browser, operating system, email client, and image editor from the last 25 years supports JPG. It's the safe, reliable choice that works everywhere.

WebP: The Modern Performance Challenger

WebP, announced by Google in 2010, was built from the ground up for the modern web. It uses advanced compression techniques based on the VP8 video codec. Unlike JPG, WebP is versatile: it supports both lossy compression (like JPG) and lossless compression (like PNG). It also supports transparency (alpha channels) and animation.

Why WebP is a Game-Changer: Its core advantage is efficiency. Google's data shows WebP lossy images are typically 25-35% smaller than equivalent-quality JPGs. This direct translation to faster loading times makes it a cornerstone of modern web performance optimization.
