How to Convert PNG to JPG: A Simple, Complete Guide

Have you ever tried to upload a PNG image to a website, only to be told the file is too large or the format isn't supported? Or maybe you have a collection of screenshots (which are often PNGs) eating up your hard drive space. You're not alone. Converting PNG files to the more universal JPG format is one of the most common digital tasks, and for good reason.

This guide is your one-stop resource for understanding why you'd want to convert these formats and, most importantly, how to do it easily using a variety of free and paid tools. We'll walk you through online converters, built-in computer software, professional programs, and even mobile apps. By the end, you'll be a PNG-to-JPG conversion pro!

PNG vs. JPG: Understanding the Key Differences

Before we jump into the "how," let's quickly cover the "why." Knowing the strengths of each format helps you decide when conversion is the right choice.

  • PNG (Portable Network Graphics): The champion of quality. PNGs use "lossless" compression, meaning no image data is lost when the file is saved. This makes them perfect for graphics with sharp edges, text, logos, and images with transparent backgrounds. The trade-off? File sizes can be quite large.
  • JPG or JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group): The master of efficiency. JPGs use "lossy" compression, which significantly reduces file size by selectively discarding some image data. This is ideal for photographs and complex images with lots of color gradients, where small quality losses aren't very noticeable. The downside? Repeated saving can degrade quality, and they don't support transparency.

When to Convert PNG to JPG: Choose JPG when you need smaller files for web use, email attachments, or saving storage space, especially for photo-like images. Stick with PNG for graphics, screenshots with text, or any image where a transparent background or perfect sharpness is non-negotiable.

Method 1: Convert PNG to JPG Using Online Tools (Fast & Easy)

Online converters are fantastic for quick, one-off jobs. You don't need to install anything; just upload, convert, and download. Here’s a general step-by-step process that applies to most sites like Convertio, Online-Convert, or ILoveIMG.

Step-by-Step Guide for Online Conversion

Step 1: Choose a reputable online converter. Search for "convert PNG to JPG" and pick a well-known service with good reviews.

Step 2: Upload your PNG file. Click the "Upload" or "Choose File" button and select the image from your computer. Many tools support drag-and-drop for even easier use.

Step 3: Adjust settings (if available). Some advanced converters let you choose the output JPG quality (usually a slider from 1-100%). A setting of 80-90% offers a great balance of size and quality.

Step 4: Initiate the conversion. Click the "Convert," "Start," or "JPG" button. The tool will process your image, which usually takes just a few seconds.

Step 5: Download your new JPG file. Once complete, a download link will appear. Click it to save the converted file to your device.

Pro Tip: Always check the privacy policy of free online tools. For highly sensitive images, consider using a downloadable software option to keep your files offline.

Method 2: Convert PNG to JPG on Your Computer (No Internet Needed)

Your computer's built-in software can handle this conversion easily, without ever opening a browser.

On Windows (Using Paint or Paint 3D)

Step 1: Right-click your PNG file and select "Open with" > Paint (the classic app) or Paint 3D.

Step 2: Once the image is open, click "File" in the top-left corner.

Step 3: Select "Save as."

Step 4: From the menu that appears, choose "JPEG picture."

Step 5: Name your file, choose a save location, and click "Save." That's it! Windows will automatically convert the PNG to a JPG.

On Mac (Using Preview)

Step 1: Double-click your PNG file to open it in Preview (the default image viewer).

Step 2: In the top menu bar, click "File" > "Export..."

Step 3: In the export dialog box, use the "Format" dropdown menu and select JPEG.

Step 4: Use the "Quality" slider to adjust the file size and compression. A higher percentage means better quality but a larger file.

Step 5: Click "Save." Your new JPG file will be saved in your chosen location.

Pro Tip: On a Mac, you can also convert images directly from the Finder. Select one or more PNG files, right-click, choose "Quick Actions," and then select "Convert Image." You can set the output format to JPEG right there.

Method 3: Using Professional Software (For Power Users)

If you need to convert batches of images or require precise control over quality, professional software is the way to go.

Adobe Photoshop

Step 1: Open your PNG file in Photoshop.

Step 2: Go to "File" > "Export" > "Save for Web (Legacy)...".

Step 3: In the dialog box, set the preset format to JPEG.

Step 4: Adjust the "Quality" setting (0-100). You'll see a live preview of the file size.

Step 5: Click "Save," choose your destination, and you're done. For batch conversion, use "File" > "Scripts" > "Image Processor."

Free Alternative: GIMP

Step 1: Open your PNG in GIMP.

Step 2: Click "File" > "Export As..."

Step 3: In the save dialog, change the file extension in the name field from ".png" to ".jpg" and click "Export."

Step 4: A new "Export Image as JPEG" window will pop up. Adjust the "Quality" slider (0-100).

Step 5: Click "Export" to finalize the conversion.

Pro Tips for the Best Conversion Results

  • Don't Over-Compress: When adjusting JPG quality, never go below 70% for web use. For print or archival, use 90-100%. The small file size savings from heavy compression aren't worth the visible quality loss (artifacts, blurriness).
  • Batch Convert for Efficiency: Dealing with dozens of images? Use software with batch processing (like Photoshop, Lightroom, or free tools like XnConvert) to convert them all at once with the same settings.
  • Beware of Transparency: PNGs can have transparent backgrounds. When you convert to JPG, that transparency will be filled with a solid color (usually white). If you need to keep a transparent background, JPG is not the right format. Stick with PNG, GIF, or WebP.
  • Keep an Original: Always save a copy of your original PNG file before converting. Once a JPG is saved with lossy compression, you cannot get that original quality data back.
