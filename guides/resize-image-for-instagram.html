Instagram quietly resizes and re-compresses every photo you upload. If your image doesn't match one of its supported dimensions, the app crops it, squeezes it, or softens it — and your carefully shot product photo or reel cover ends up looking worse than the original. The fix is simple: resize to the exact size Instagram expects before you upload. This guide lists every current Instagram dimension for 2026 and shows you how to hit it in about 30 seconds.

Instagram image size cheat sheet (2026)

Instagram displays uploaded photos at a maximum of 1080 pixels wide, so there is no benefit to uploading anything wider. Use these exact dimensions:

Why aspect ratio matters more than pixels

Instagram cares about the shape of your image first. If you upload a 4000×3000 landscape photo to a feed that expects 4:5, Instagram will either crop the sides off or letterbox it. Resizing to the correct pixel size is only half the job — the image must also be the right ratio:

  • 1:1 — equal width and height (square).
  • 4:5 — slightly taller than wide (portrait feed).
  • 9:16 — tall and narrow (Stories & Reels, full screen).
  • 1.91:1 — wide landscape (the maximum width Instagram allows in feed).

If your original is the wrong shape, crop it to the target ratio first, then resize. This gives you control over what stays in frame instead of letting Instagram decide.

The Stories & Reels "safe zone"

A Story or Reel is 1080×1920, but the app covers parts of it with interface elements. To keep captions, logos, and faces visible:

  • Leave the top ~250 px clear (profile name and close button).
  • Leave the bottom ~250 px clear (caption, "Send message", and Reels controls).
  • Place key content in the central 1080 × 1420 px safe zone.

Common mistakes that ruin Instagram photos

  • Uploading huge files: a 5–8 MB photo gets aggressively re-compressed. Resize to 1080 px wide and the result is sharper, not softer.
  • Resizing a small image up: enlarging a 600 px photo to 1080 px invents pixels and looks blurry. Always start from the largest original you have.
  • Wrong ratio: a square crop of a portrait subject chops off heads. Crop to ratio deliberately.
  • Screenshotting instead of exporting: screenshots add phone-specific dimensions and UI. Export a clean file at the exact size.
  • Forgetting the safe zone on Stories, so text sits under the caption bar.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best image size for an Instagram post in 2026?

Use 1080×1080 px for a square, or 1080×1350 px (4:5 portrait) for the most feed coverage and engagement. Landscape is 1080×566 px. Instagram caps display width at 1080 px, so there's no need to upload larger.

What size should Instagram Stories and Reels be?

Both are 1080×1920 px (9:16 vertical). Keep important content in the central safe zone, away from the top and bottom ~250 px the interface covers.

Why does Instagram make my photos blurry?

It re-compresses images that don't match a supported size or are far larger than 1080 px wide. Resize to an exact supported dimension and export a JPEG at 80–90% quality before uploading.

Does resizing reduce my image quality?

Resizing a large original down to 1080 px keeps quality high. Enlarging a small image looks soft. Always start from the highest-resolution source.
